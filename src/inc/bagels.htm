Bagels

Overview

It's an easy recipe, but it's in two parts:

  1. Mix the dough, rest, shape, refrigerate overnight
  2. Boil, drain, add toppings, bake

Ingredients

Makes 10 bagels

  • 680 grams of high gluten flour
  • 2 tsp table salt
  • 3/4 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 Tbs Diastatic yeast powder
  • 1 1/2 cup + 1 Tbs warm water

Instructions

  • Dissolve the malt and yeast in water, stir and let sit 5 mins to ensure activation.
  • Blend flour and salt.
  • Add water and blend/knead for ~8 mins until the dough is smooth and stretchy.
  • Roll into two 2" logs, cover and let rest for 20 mins.
  • Cut into 10 equal parts, stretch around you fingers and roll into bagel shape.
  • Put bagels on tray, cover and refrigerate overnight.
  • Mix 2 Tbs malt powder with 2 Tbs water to make a slurry.
  • Boil wide pot of water, add the slurry to it, and also preheat oven to 450Â°F.
  • Boil the bagels in batches that fit the pot you're using until they float or ~4 mins. Drain on a rack and add toppings if you like.
  • Bake 15+ mins, let cool for 30.

Bonus tips

  • Use "Super Bagel Flour" from Baker's Authority
  • Not all Diastatic malt powder is equal, and it's a major flavor component
  • Equal sized bagels are nicer, use a scale to divide the weight of the dough as you cut
  • Use a pizza stone for a nice crust on the bottom (I prefer this and don't flip during the bake)
